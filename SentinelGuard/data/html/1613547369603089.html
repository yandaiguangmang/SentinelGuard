Update: damaged sailing yacht My Song hauled out in Palma

3 June 2019 17:00
Fleet
Written by
Charl van Rooy

The 40-metre racing sailing yacht  My Song that was damaged last week after it fell overboard from the transport ship it was travelling on arrived in Palma de Mallorca today where it was hauled out of the water for the first time since the incident. Images from local sources reveal a badly damaged My Song hauled out hull with its hull having sustained serious damage. Photo: SYT Reader Photo: SYT Reader

Baltic Yachts , who launched the yacht at its shipyard in 2016, is known to have a repair facility in Palma de Mallorca and are  “standing by to offer all assistance requested,” according to Baltic CEO, Henry Hawkins. “We are all devastated by this tragic accident and our thoughts are with the client at this time.” Photo: SYT Reader

Not commenting on the fate of the vessel at this time, Hawkins confirmed that it will now be up to the insurance company and the yacht’s owner to determine the next step in the process after careful considerations have been made. Photo: SYT Reader Photo: SYT Reader

My Song is owned by Pier Luigi Loro Piana and was due to appear at the Loro Piana Superyacht Regatta this week. She was built to a design of Nauta Yachts and Reichel / Pugh  Yacht Design . Photo: Merijn de Waard / SuperYacht Times
